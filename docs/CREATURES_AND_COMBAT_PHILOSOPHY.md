## PURPOSE

This document defines the living threats of Aetheria. Combat is not about saving the world or grinding for levels; it is about surviving an untamed, feral wilderness. Creatures are categorized by their ecological role, ensuring every encounter feels grounded, believable, and geographically appropriate.

---

### 1. Wildlife (Natural Environmental Threats)

Standard beasts that inhabit the wilds, acting as natural friction during long-distance travel. They hunt for food, not out of malice.

* **Examples:** Highland Wolves, Tusked Boars, River-Mouth Snappers, Blind Cave Crawlers.
* **Behavior:** Instinctual. Wolves hunt in flanking packs; boars charge linearly; snappers ambush from the water's edge. They will flee if intimidated with fire or heavy damage.
* **Habitat:** Open roads, forest borders, and shallow rivers.
* **Combat Role:** Resource drainers. They test the player's basic combat reflexes and force them to expend stamina or minor healing items.
* **Rewards:** Basic survival materials (Meat for cooking, Leather for armor patching, Bone for tool crafting).
* **Relationship to the World:** They are the foundation of the food chain. Wayfinders respect them but cull them when they encroach too closely on established roads.

### 2. Territorial Creatures (Resource Guardians)

Magically or environmentally adapted beasts that have grown massive and stubborn. They do not hunt; they fiercely defend their immediate surroundings.

* **Examples:** Bramble Crabs (crabs using petrified wood as shells), Root Serpents, Mossbacks (giant snapping turtles with camouflaged shells).
* **Behavior:** Defensive and slow, but highly lethal up close. They possess heavy natural armor and immunity to standard stagger effects.
* **Habitat:** High-value gathering nodes, ancient ruins, and crucial geographical chokepoints (like narrow mountain passes).
* **Combat Role:** Gear and tactical checks. Players must use heavy weapons (Hammers) to break their armor, or specific tools (fire) to bypass their defenses.
* **Rewards:** Rare crafting materials (Impenetrable Carapace, Iron-Root fiber) necessary for mid-to-late game gear upgrades.
* **Relationship to the World:** They act as natural gates to progression, keeping unprepared Wayfinders out of dangerous territories.

### 3. Dungeon Threats (Puzzle & Isolation Combat)

Entities isolated to deep caves, ruins, and the ancient Brass-Lines. They are distinctly unnatural or deeply mutated by localized conditions.

* **Examples:** Rusted Brass Automatons, Observatory Sentinels, Subterranean Fungal Hulks.
* **Behavior:** Relentless and predictable. Automatons follow strict patrol paths and attack mechanically; Fungal Hulks mindlessly spread toxic AoE clouds.
* **Habitat:** Dungeons, deep ruins, and sealed environments.
* **Combat Role:** High-threat encounters intertwined with environmental puzzles. Players may need to lure an Automaton onto a pressure plate or use a Fungal Hulk's explosive spores to break a cracked wall.
* **Rewards:** Ancient technology (Brass Gears, Aether-Cores) and exotic alchemy ingredients (Luminous Spores).
* **Relationship to the World:** Remnants of a forgotten age or extreme biological isolation, serving as stark contrasts to the cozy surface world.

### 4. Apex Predators (Optional High-Risk Encounters)

Legendary, singular beasts that rule their respective biomes. These are not magical raid bosses or evil dragons, but rather ancient, massive forces of nature.

* **Examples:** The Verdant Stag (Whispering Woods), The Deep-Loch Leviathan (Glimmering Loch), The Canyon Goliath (Heartlands).
* **Behavior:** Complex, multi-stage combat requiring mastery of dodging, parrying, and terrain exploitation.
* **Habitat:** Hidden, hard-to-reach arenas deep within the far corners of major regions.
* **Combat Role:** The ultimate test of a Wayfinder's preparation, gear, and combat mechanics. Highly optional.
* **Rewards:** Unique titles (e.g., "Stag-Breaker"), legendary salvage used to craft the highest tier of aesthetic travel gear, and massive regional reputation boosts.
* **Relationship to the World:** Revered by locals in myths and tavern songs. They represent the untamed majesty of Aetheria.

# docs/WEAPONS_AND_EQUIPMENT.md

## PURPOSE

This document outlines the tools of the Wayfinder trade. Aetheria eschews oversized fantasy armor and magical swords in favor of practical, modular, and grounded travel gear. Weapons double as exploration tools, and armor is designed to survive the elements, not just deflect blows.

---

### 1. Weapons (Dual-Utility)

Weapons in LOA are specialized tools with distinct combat roles and environmental interactions.

* **Short Sword:** Fast, low stamina cost. *Exploration:* Instantly slices through thick hanging vines and foliage without breaking stride.
* **Hatchet:** High stagger, brutal crits on unarmored targets. *Exploration:* Highly efficient woodcutting, yielding 2x timber from fallen logs.
* **Spear:** High reach, tight hitbox for narrow corridors. *Exploration:* Prods deep water for submerged items or safely triggers floor traps from a distance.
* **Hammer:** Destroys enemy armor, ignores shields. *Exploration:* Smashes cracked masonry to reveal hidden rooms; drives in bridge-repair pitons with one strike.
* **Great Axe:** Massive AoE cleave, slow swing. *Exploration:* The only tool capable of chopping down massive, petrified root-barriers.
* **Bow:** High ranged damage, stealth utility. *Exploration:* Shoots hanging ropes to drop drawbridges or triggers distant wall-switches.
* **Sling:** Fast, allows movement while firing, uses free rocks. *Exploration:* Knocks rare fruit out of trees or creates noise distractions in the brush.

### 2. Tools (The Wayfinder Kit)

Tools do not deal damage; they manipulate the world and enable survival.

* **Brass Lantern:** Illuminates dark spaces, repels nocturnal predators, and reveals aether-ink clues.
* **Hemp Rope:** Used to create permanent shortcuts by anchoring descent lines down cliffsides.
* **Climbing Kit:** Pitons and chalk that passively reduce stamina drain when scaling vertical surfaces.
* **Compass:** An upgradable UI tool that points toward tracked landmarks or quest objectives.
* **Spyglass:** Allows the player to zoom in on the horizon and permanently mark discovered landmarks on their map from afar.
* **Survey Kit:** Required for sketching maps at high-altitude vantage points to sell to the Wayfinder Registry.

### 3. Armor & Travel Gear

There is no plate mail or spiked pauldrons. Gear is categorized into functional clothing designed for a grueling journey.

* **Coats/Cloaks:** Govern weather resistance (cold, rain, wind). A heavy wool coat prevents stamina drain in the mountain passes.
* **Scarves/Masks:** Govern hazard resistance. A treated leather mask prevents poisoning from fungal spores.
* **Boots:** Govern movement speed and terrain traction. Spiked boots prevent slipping on mud during rainstorms.
* **Gloves:** Govern gathering speed and climbing efficiency.
* **Backpacks (Core Accessory):** Govern total inventory slots and carry weight.

### 4. Equipment Progression

Players do not find "Sword +1" in a chest. Progression relies on **Craftsmanship**.

1. **Gathering:** Harvest raw materials (Iron-root, Bramble Carapace, Aether-Silk).
2. **Commissioning:** Take materials to specialized town NPCs (Emberforge Blacksmith, Mistwood Weaver).
3. **Reinforcing:** Base items are visually and mechanically upgraded (e.g., Canvas Coat -> Wax-Treated Coat -> Aether-Weave Overcoat).

# docs/TRAVEL_AND_MOUNTS.md

## PURPOSE

Travel is the gameplay. This document defines the friction, joy, and progression of moving across Aetheria. It outlines how players navigate the world on foot, utilize pack animals, and eventually earn rideable mounts without trivializing the 3DS map scale.

---

### 1. On-Foot Travel (The Core Loop)

* **Roads:** The safest paths. Traveling on cobblestone or packed dirt slightly increases movement speed and drastically reduces enemy spawns.
* **Shortcuts:** Diegetic progression. Players spend wood/rope to repair bridges or kick down ladders, permanently altering the map to make backtracking faster.
* **Camps:** Mid-route checkpoints. Players must gather wood to build a fire to cook food, restore stamina, and advance time.
* **Weather:** Dynamic friction. Rain makes cliffs unclimbable, extinguishes unprotected campfires, and causes rivers to swell, forcing players to find alternate routes.

### 2. Pack Animals (Burden Beasts)

Players can purchase or rent non-rideable companion animals early in the game to manage inventory.

* **Examples:** The Aetherian Yak, the Burrow-Beetle.
* **Function:** They follow the player automatically along safe roads and carry massive amounts of crafting materials.
* **Limitations:** They cannot climb cliffs, enter dungeons, or cross unstable bridges. If ambushed, the player must defend them, or the beast will flee back to the nearest town, dropping its cargo.

### 3. Rideable Mounts (Version 1 Scope)

Earned in the late game (Act 3/4), rideable mounts are designed to respect the limited hardware scope of the 3DS and the handcrafted level design.

* **The Ridge-Goat:** A sturdy, sure-footed beast.
* **Function:** Faster than sprinting on roads, but its true utility is completely ignoring mud/snow movement penalties and jumping over small gaps.
* **Limitations:** It is wide and cannot fit through dense forest brush, dungeons, or narrow caves. It does not fly. It bounds the player to the main roads they have previously repaired, acting as a reward for their infrastructure work.

### 4. Mount Progression

Mounts are customizable companions, not vehicles.

* **Saddlebags:** Expand the mount's inventory capacity.
* **Upgraded Horseshoes:** Reduce the mount's stamina drain when sprinting.
* **Lantern Hooks:** Attaching a Brass-Lamp to the saddle allows safe, illuminated travel at night without holding a torch.

### 5. Caravans

NPC-driven economic lifelines that travel between connected towns.

* **Operation:** Once a player repairs a road connecting two towns, NPC merchant caravans will begin traveling it in real-time.
* **Player Interaction:** Players can escort them for minor rewards, trade with them on the road for unique regional goods, or pay a fee to ride in the wagon, acting as the game's immersive, diegetic **Fast Travel** system between safe zones.

### 6. Travel Philosophy

**Why earn a mount?**
A mount is the ultimate reward for a Master Wayfinder. Once you have painstakingly repaired the Grand Route, the mount allows you to traverse your completed handiwork with speed and pride, shifting the late-game focus from surviving the roads to managing a continent-wide network.

**Why does walking remain valuable?**
Mounts and caravans are restricted to main roads. To find hidden caves, gather rare off-road herbs, hunt apex predators, or survey from mountain peaks, the player must dismount. Walking remains the exclusive method for true discovery, ensuring the world never feels small or skipped over.
