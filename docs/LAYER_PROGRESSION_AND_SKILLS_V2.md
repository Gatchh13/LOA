## REVISION 1: CARTOGRAPHY EXPANSION

*Cartography has been redesigned from a passive map-reveal mechanic into a fully active, lucrative, and engaging profession.*

* **Cartography**
* *Levels by:* Surveying landmarks from high vantage points, submitting hand-sketched regional maps to the Aethercrest Registry, and fulfilling Lodge "Survey Contracts."
* *Benefits:* Transforms the map into an interactive tool, unlocks cartographic income, and reveals hidden environmental layers.
* *Milestones:*
* **(25) Surveyor's Eye:** Sketching major landmarks from designated vantage points grants a "Clarity" stamina regeneration buff. Completed regional map copies can now be sold to Lodge Masters for currency.
* **(50) Hidden Paths:** The Cartography UI now detects and displays faint dotted lines where overgrown, forgotten Brass-Line shortcuts or hidden caves exist, encouraging off-road exploration.
* **(75) Contract Cartographer:** Unlocks high-value "Survey Contracts" at Lodges (e.g., mapping hazardous, toxic zones for a local town). Fulfilling these grants massive regional affinity.
* **(100) Master Waylines:** Maps automatically update with real-time weather patterns and beast migrations. Sold maps are officially recognized by the Registry, generating a passive weekly "royalties" income.

## REVISION 2 & 3: SOCIAL SKILLS TWEAKS (PERSUASION & NEGOTIATION)

*Level 100 milestones have been grounded to preserve narrative consequences and economic stability.*

* **Persuasion**
* *Levels by:* De-escalating tense NPC situations, successfully gathering intel.
* *Benefits:* Unlocks hidden dialogue branches and better quest rewards.
* *Milestones:*
* (25) View NPC emotional states.
* (50) Bypass minor quest gates.
* (75) Request emergency supplies from towns.
* **(100) Master Mediator:** Major reputation losses from failed quests or negative interactions are halved. Unlocks "De-escalation" dialogue choices that can turn historically hostile NPC factions (like smugglers or isolated splinter-groups) into neutral contacts.




* **Negotiation**
* *Levels by:* Buying and selling goods at markets.
* *Benefits:* Improves buy/sell ratios and trade network manipulation.
* *Milestones:*
* (25) Sell prices +10%.
* (50) Buy prices -15%.
* (75) Access merchant hidden inventories.
* **(100) Trade Route Director:** Allows the player to establish automated "Caravan Contracts" between two towns with high affinity. This generates a passive, recurring delivery of region-specific materials directly to the player's Lodge stash.

## REVISION 4: COMMUNITY SKILL EXPANSION

*Community has been elevated to a major progression pillar, allowing the player to act as a civic leader who physically alters towns through public works.*

* **Community**
* *Levels by:* Funding public works, upgrading regional Lodges, fulfilling festival preparation tasks, and repairing critical town infrastructure.
* *Benefits:* Unlocks massive, permanent visual and mechanical upgrades to the world's safe zones.
* *Milestones:*
* **(25) Local Benefactor:** Can contribute raw materials to "Town Projects" via the Mayor's ledger (e.g., upgrading a town's well to grant water buffs, or repairing the local mill to lower bread prices).
* **(50) Lodge Architect:** Unlocks the ability to visually and mechanically upgrade Wayfinder Lodges. Players can build crafting benches, expand the communal stash, and establish fast-travel courier pigeon roosts.
* **(75) Festival Coordinator:** Can initiate seasonal festivals in high-affinity towns by providing rare regional goods. Festivals temporarily boost all local merchants' inventories and remove negative weather effects in the region for three in-game days.
* **(100) Regional Restoration:** Unlocks access to massive, region-wide restoration projects (e.g., restoring the Grand Aqueduct or repairing the Loch's main dam). Completing these permanently alters the biome, opens new safe trade highways, and reduces enemy spawn rates in the restored zone.

## REVISION 5: WEAPON IDENTITY AND UTILITY

*Combat skills (One-Handed, Two-Handed, Ranged) govern statistical growth, but specific weapon types now dictate gameplay style, pacing, and exploration utility. Weapons are not cosmetic reskins; they are distinct tools.*

### 1. Short Sword (One-Handed)

* **Strengths:** Extremely fast attack speed, low stamina drain, easy to parry with.
* **Weaknesses:** Low stagger potential; struggles against armored targets (like automaton crabs).
* **Intended Users:** Couriers and lightweight travelers who need to defend themselves quickly and retreat.
* **Exploration Utility:** Slices through thick hanging vines and foliage instantly without halting player momentum.

### 2. Hatchet (One-Handed)

* **Strengths:** High stagger chance, brutal critical hits on unarmored targets.
* **Weaknesses:** Very short reach; leaves the player exposed if they miss.
* **Intended Users:** Trail-Menders and foragers who pack light but need reliable stopping power against beasts.
* **Exploration Utility:** Highly efficient woodcutting. Yields 2x timber from fallen logs compared to swords or raw gathering.

### 3. Spear (Two-Handed)

* **Strengths:** Exceptional reach; narrow hitbox allows for fighting in tight corridors. High piercing damage.
* **Weaknesses:** Poor crowd control; useless if enemies close the distance or surround the player.
* **Intended Users:** Cautious explorers and town guards keeping beasts at bay.
* **Exploration Utility:** Can be used to safely prod deep water for submerged items, or poke pressure plates/traps from a safe distance.

### 4. Hammer (Two-Handed)

* **Strengths:** Massive armor destruction; ignores enemy carapace or shield mitigation.
* **Weaknesses:** Incredibly slow swing speed; missing a swing drains significant stamina.
* **Intended Users:** Ruin-delvers and mechanics facing ancient Brass-Line automatons.
* **Exploration Utility:** Smashes cracked masonry to reveal hidden rooms. Can drive in bridge-repair pitons in one strike instead of three.

### 5. Great Axe (Two-Handed)

* **Strengths:** Huge sweeping area-of-effect (AoE); can cleave multiple enemies at once.
* **Weaknesses:** Heaviest weapon class; severely penalizes movement speed and dodge distance when drawn.
* **Intended Users:** Heavy-combat focused players clearing out dense monster dens.
* **Exploration Utility:** The only tool capable of chopping down massive, petrified root-barriers that block late-game shortcuts.

### 6. Bow (Ranged)

* **Strengths:** High damage, extreme range, allows for stealth takedowns.
* **Weaknesses:** Requires crafted ammunition (arrows); slow draw time makes it a liability in close quarters.
* **Intended Users:** Hunters, scouts, and players who prefer to thin out enemy packs before engaging.
* **Exploration Utility:** Can shoot hanging ropes to drop drawbridges from afar, or trigger distant wall-switches in dungeons.

### 7. Sling (Ranged)

* **Strengths:** Infinite free ammunition (rocks gathered from the ground); very fast wind-up; allows movement while firing.
* **Weaknesses:** Very low damage; poor armor penetration.
* **Intended Users:** Scavengers and pacifist players who use ranged combat for utility rather than killing.
* **Exploration Utility:** Ideal for knocking rare fruit off high branches, or firing into the brush to create noise distractions and lure territorial beasts away from the main path.
