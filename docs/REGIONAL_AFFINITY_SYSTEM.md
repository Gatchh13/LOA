PURPOSE
This document details the Regional Affinity System, which serves as the primary social and narrative progression engine of Legends of Aetheria (LOA). Traditional RPGs reward players for clearing out monster camps; LOA rewards players for putting the world back together. This system ensures that every act of exploration, repair, and delivery directly impacts the physical world and how communities treat the player.

1. SOURCES OF AFFINITY (THE WAYFINDER LOOP)
Affinity is earned through acts of connection and community service. Combat kills grant zero Affinity. (Killing a Badger-Boar gives you meat; fixing the fence the Badger-Boar broke gives you Affinity).

Players build trust through:

Deliveries: Carrying letters, parcels, and perishable goods (like Mistwood herbs) between towns.

Infrastructure Repair: Rebuilding local bridges, clearing blocked mountain passes, and kicking down permanent shortcut ladders in the region.

Cartography: Turning in completed regional maps and sketched landmarks to the local Lodge.

Community Projects: Donating gathered materials (e.g., 50x Iron-Root Wood, 20x Turtle-Crab Shells) to a Mayor's ledger to fund a town upgrade.

Festival Preparations: Gathering unique regional materials (like Koi-Goat milk or Stag-Moth velvet) requested by townsfolk for seasonal events.

2. AFFINITY TIERS AND SOCIAL RECOGNITION
Every V1 settlement tracks the player's Affinity across four distinct tiers. NPCs dynamically shift their behavior as the player progresses.

Tier 1: Stranger

Recognition: NPCs use generic greetings ("Watch the roads," "Careful in the woods").

Economy: Standard prices.

Tier 2: Familiar Face

Recognition: NPCs recognize the player's profession. ("Ah, the new Wayfinder," "Thanks for fixing the south bridge.")

Economy: 10% discount at local merchants.

Tier 3: Trusted Wayfinder

Recognition: NPCs use the player's name. They share personal stories and local rumors that lead to hidden map locations.

Economy: Access to merchants' hidden inventories (rare crafting materials).

Tier 4: Hearth-Kin

Recognition: The ultimate level of trust. The player is invited to sit in the tavern's "Hearth-Seat" (traditionally reserved for honored guests).

Economy: Inn stays and basic meals are permanently free.

3. SETTLEMENT SPECIFICS & VISIBLE GROWTH
As Affinity increases, the town physically evolves. The player should visually see the impact of their hard work.

Oakhaven (The Verdant Cradle)
Theme: Agriculture and Beginnings.

Visible Growth: The broken Giant's Windmill is repaired and visibly turns. New market stalls open selling a wider variety of crops. Pens are built for domesticated Hare-Deer.

Tier 4 Reward: Mayor Nora grants the player the key to the Old Mill, acting as a personal player house/storage hub in the starting region.

Story Impact: Oakhaven farmers begin patrolling the Cradle's roads, making the starting region almost entirely safe from territorial wildlife.

Mistwood Village (The Whispering Woods)
Theme: Foraging, Seclusion, and Nature.

Visible Growth: Thick vines covering the village are pruned. New woven suspension bridges connect the higher canopy treehouses. Glowing moss lanterns are hung, changing the village lighting from dim to vibrantly warm at night.

Tier 4 Reward: Weaver Elara teaches the player the "Canopy Step" technique, passively reducing stamina drain when climbing wooden surfaces by 30%.

Story Impact: Mistwood foragers will occasionally leave rare herbs and potions in the player's Lodge stash as a thank-you.

Lapis Cove (Glimmering Loch)
Theme: Fishing, Tides, and Trade.

Visible Growth: Dilapidated stilt-houses are reinforced. The docks expand significantly. Koi-Goat pens are upgraded, and newly built ferry boats bob in the water.

Tier 4 Reward: Ferryman Finn grants the player the "Master's Lure," permanently increasing the catch rate of rare fish across all of Aetheria.

Story Impact: Free, instant ferry travel is unlocked between Lapis Cove and any restored dock in the Loch.

Aethercrest (Aetheria Heartlands)
Theme: Bureaucracy, Craftsmanship, and The Capital.

Visible Growth: The tarnished Brass-Lines running through the city are polished to a brilliant gold. The Grand Water Clock begins moving. Merchant caravans from Oakhaven, Mistwood, and Lapis Cove physically appear in the city squares.

Tier 4 Reward: Archivist Linnea grants the player the "Aethercrest Master-Key," unlocking shortcuts through the city's aqueducts to bypass the winding streets.

Story Impact: The player gains the authority to automatically approve other NPC Wayfinders' maps at the Registry.

4. DYNAMIC WORLD CONNECTIVITY
The Affinity system also tracks Inter-Regional Affinity.

As the player maxes out trust in multiple towns, the world blends together:

Cross-Pollination: At high Affinity, you will see Mistwood foragers buying bread in Oakhaven, or Lapis Cove fishers selling pearls in Aethercrest.

Caravan Routes: Repairing the roads is only half the battle. Once two connected towns both reach Tier 3 Affinity, a permanent NPC caravan begins traveling between them, which the player can ride or use as a mobile shop.

5. THE MASTER WAYFINDER RECOGNITION (V1 CAPSTONE)
Achieving Tier 4 (Hearth-Kin) in all four Version 1 settlements is the ultimate optional completionist goal of the game. It represents a fully reconnected world.

Requirements: Max Affinity in Oakhaven, Mistwood Village, Lapis Cove, and Aethercrest.

The Rewards:

Unique Title: "The Continent's Thread" (Equippable title that causes traveling NPCs to salute or bow slightly when passed on the road).

Cosmetic Reward: The Aether-Weave Cloak. A beautifully tailored, faintly glowing overcoat woven from materials from all four regions. Note: It offers identical weather protection to standard end-game gear. It provides zero combat advantage. It is purely a badge of immense prestige.

Special Ending Dialogue: During the Centennial Lantern Festival finale, if this status is achieved, Elder Elias and the four settlement leaders will pull the player aside for a secret, deeply emotional joint toast before the bell rings, explicitly acknowledging that the player didn't just fix the roads—they saved the soul of Aetheria.
