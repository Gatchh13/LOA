PURPOSE
This document details the Regional Affinity System, which serves as the primary social and narrative progression engine of Legends of Aetheria (LOA). Traditional RPGs reward players for clearing out monster camps; LOA rewards players for putting the world back together. This system ensures that every act of exploration, repair, and delivery directly impacts the physical world and how communities treat the player.

Core Rule: No Affinity Decay
A common flaw in RPGs is punishing players for leaving a zone. In LOA, Affinity never decays. Period. Once a community trusts you, they trust you forever. The player should never feel anxious about losing progress just because they traveled to the other side of the continent for an in-game month.

1. SOURCES OF AFFINITY (THE WAYFINDER LOOP)
Affinity is earned through acts of connection and community service. Combat kills grant zero Affinity. (Killing a Badger-Boar gives you meat; fixing the fence the Badger-Boar broke gives you Affinity).

Players build trust through:

Deliveries: Carrying letters, parcels, and perishable goods between towns.

Cartography: Turning in completed regional maps and sketched landmarks to the local Lodge.

Festival Preparations: Gathering unique regional materials requested by townsfolk for seasonal events.

Community Projects: The signature Affinity mechanic. Major infrastructure and town upgrades funded and built by the player (detailed below).

2. AFFINITY TIERS AND SOCIAL RECOGNITION
Every V1 settlement tracks the player's Affinity across four distinct tiers. NPCs dynamically shift their behavior as the player progresses. Crucially, high tiers unlock emotional and experiential rewards, not combat power. A player who ignores Affinity is not objectively weaker in combat or exploration—they simply miss out on the emotional warmth of the world.

Tier 1: Stranger

Recognition: NPCs use generic greetings ("Watch the roads," "Careful in the woods").

Economy: Standard prices.

Tier 2: Familiar Face

Recognition: NPCs recognize the player's profession. ("Ah, the new Wayfinder," "Thanks for fixing the south bridge.")

Economy: 10% discount at local merchants.

Tier 3: Trusted Wayfinder

Recognition: NPCs use the player's name. They share personal stories and local rumors that lead to hidden map locations.

Experience: Affinity unlocks participation in the town's unique Regional Festival.

Tier 4: Hearth-Kin

Recognition: The ultimate level of trust. The player is treated like family.

Reward: Unique cosmetic, housing, or convenience items. (No stat-boosting gear or combat advantages).

3. SETTLEMENT SPECIFICS, PROJECTS, AND FESTIVALS
As Affinity increases, the town physically evolves. The player should visually see the impact of their hard work, akin to city-building elements in Dark Cloud or Animal Crossing, but on an intimate, handcrafted scale.

Oakhaven (The Verdant Cradle)
Theme: Agriculture and Beginnings.

Community Projects:

Repair the Windmill: The massive blades begin turning again, visible from anywhere in the region.

Build a Greenhouse: Adds a beautiful glass structure to the town where rare herbs are cultivated.

Expand the Market: New stalls open with colorful awnings, bustling with traveling merchants.

Regional Festival: The Harvest Fair. Affinity unlocks participation in pumpkin-weighing contests, cooking minigames, and a beautiful evening event where children run through the fields with paper lanterns.

Tier 4 Reward: Mayor Nora grants the key to the Old Mill, acting as a personal player house and storage hub. Farmers permanently greet the player by name when passed on the road. Additional cosmetic upgrades for the house are unlocked.

Mistwood Village (The Whispering Woods)
Theme: Foraging, Seclusion, and Nature.

Community Projects:

New Canopy Bridge: Physically connects two previously separated giant tree-hubs.

Herb Garden: A suspended, terraced garden woven from vines is constructed.

Lodge Expansion: The local Wayfinder Lodge is upgraded from a small tent into a beautiful wooden structure.

Regional Festival: Night of Mosslights. The entire village is illuminated by cultivated glowing moss. The player can sit around the fire for unique music, folklore storytelling, and communal dancing.

Tier 4 Reward: Weaver Elara gifts a ceremonial Mistwood Cloak. It features a stunning, unique woven appearance to show the player's bond with the forest. It provides zero gameplay stats or bonuses.

Lapis Cove (Glimmering Loch)
Theme: Fishing, Tides, and Trade.

Community Projects:

Dock Reconstruction: Dilapidated wooden planks are replaced with wide, sturdy piers.

Fishing Pier: A dedicated jutting pier is built where old fishers gather to cast lines.

Ferry Station: A small, bustling ticket booth and waiting deck are constructed for Loch travelers.

Regional Festival: Moon Tide Festival. Dozens of small boats covered in lanterns float out onto the Loch. Unlocks fishing contests and a vibrant, limited-time Night Market.

Tier 4 Reward: Ferryman Finn carves the player a handcrafted Fishing Rod Skin. It features beautiful pearl inlays and brass wrappings. It has no gameplay effect and does not increase catch rates; it is a purely cosmetic badge of honor.

Aethercrest (Aetheria Heartlands)
Theme: Bureaucracy, Craftsmanship, and The Capital.

Community Projects:

Brass-Line Restoration: The tarnished pipes running through the city squares are polished to a brilliant gold and vent harmless, warm steam.

Registry Archives: The cluttered Lodge is expanded into a multi-floor library of neatly organized maps.

Clocktower Repairs: The gears are cleaned, and the tower's face visually tracks real in-game time.

Regional Festival: Centennial Lantern Festival. The climax of the main story. Thousands of lanterns are released over the capital.

Tier 4 Reward: Archivist Linnea grants the "Aethercrest Master-Key." This unlocks underground aqueduct grates, acting as a traversal shortcut to conveniently bypass the winding, multi-tiered streets of the capital city.

4. THE MASTER WAYFINDER RECOGNITION (V1 CAPSTONE)
Achieving Tier 4 (Hearth-Kin) in all four Version 1 settlements is the ultimate completionist goal of the game. It represents a fully reconnected world and provides massive emotional payoff rather than mechanical power.

Requirements: Max Affinity in Oakhaven, Mistwood Village, Lapis Cove, and Aethercrest.

The Rewards:

The Fifth Hearth: The most profound cultural honor in Aetheria. A beautifully carved, permanent chair appears beside Elder Elias' fireplace in Oakhaven. The player now has their own Hearth-Seat. Traveling NPCs will occasionally mention it with awe ("I saw the Fifth Hearth... you really walked the whole world, didn't you?"). No gameplay bonus; massive emotional reward.

Unique Title: "The Continent's Thread" (Equippable title that causes traveling NPCs to salute or bow slightly when passed on the road).

The Aether-Weave Cloak: A beautifully tailored overcoat woven from materials from all four regions. It offers identical weather protection to standard end-game gear. It provides zero combat advantage.

Special Ending Dialogue: During the Centennial Lantern Festival finale, Elder Elias and the four settlement leaders will pull the player aside for a secret, deeply emotional joint toast, explicitly acknowledging that the player didn't just fix the roads—they saved the soul of Aetheria.
