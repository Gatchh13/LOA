## PURPOSE

This document establishes the ecological laws of Aetheria. To create a deeply memorable and unique world, LOA completely abandons standard Earth animals and generic fantasy monsters (dragons, goblins). Instead, Aetheria is populated entirely by **Hybrid Wildlife**—creatures that naturally evolved combining the physical and behavioral traits of two recognizable animals.

## ECOLOGICAL DESIGN RULES

1. **No Earth Animals:** If it exists in the real world, it does not exist in Aetheria. There are no normal horses, dogs, or rabbits.
2. **No Fantasy Monsters:** No elemental Pokémon, no undead, no reality-warping magical beasts.
3. **The Silhouette Rule:** A player should be able to identify a creature's behavior and threat level purely from its silhouette (e.g., the bulky front of a badger combined with the tusks of a boar screams "territorial bruiser").
4. **Ecological Niches:** Every creature must make sense in its environment. A creature with heron legs must live near shallow water.

## WILDLIFE CLASSIFICATIONS

* **Common Wildlife (Passive):** Flee when approached. They exist to make the world feel alive, provide basic gathering materials (via hunting or observing), and serve as atmospheric set dressing.
* **Territorial Wildlife (Defensive):** Will aggressively protect their space, nests, or food sources. They form the bulk of LOA's combat encounters.
* **Apex Wildlife (Mini-Bosses):** Rare, ancient, and massive variants of standard creatures. High-risk, optional encounters.

## CULTURAL INTEGRATION

Creatures are deeply woven into daily life. They are not just targets; they are livestock, pests, pets, and food.

* Inns serve localized dishes (e.g., Stag-Moth mushroom stew).
* Town bulletin boards feature bounties on aggressive pests (e.g., Badger-Boars digging up crop fields).
* Merchants use domesticated hybrids to pull their carts.

# docs/REGIONAL_FAUNA.md

## PURPOSE

A catalog of the primary hybrid species populating the four Version 1 regions.

### 1. The Verdant Cradle

* **Hare-Deer (Common)**
* *Anatomy:* Body of a small deer, long rabbit ears, powerful hind legs, and small velvet antlers.
* *Behavior:* Extremely fast, skittish herbivores.
* *Culture:* Frequently seen grazing near Oakhaven's farms. Children often try to sneak up on them.


* **Badger-Boar (Territorial)**
* *Anatomy:* The low, wide, striped body of a badger with the snout and sharp tusks of a boar.
* *Behavior:* Highly aggressive scavengers. They will charge Wayfinders who get too close.
* *Culture:* The bane of Cradle farmers; they routinely dig up pumpkin patches.


* **Finch-Squirrel (Common)**
* *Anatomy:* A squirrel with feathered, finch-like wings instead of skin flaps, and a beak for cracking nuts.
* *Behavior:* Glides between the autumnal trees of the Cradle.



### 2. The Whispering Woods

* **Stag-Moth (Common/Passive)**
* *Anatomy:* A large, gentle quadruped. Its antlers resemble branching wood, and a thick mane of moth-like fuzz covers its neck. It has large, vestigial moth wings it fans out to display mood.
* *Behavior:* Grazes on bioluminescent fungi. Moves very slowly.
* *Culture:* Shed Stag-Moth velvet is a highly prized weaving material in Mistwood Village.


* **Owl-Fox (Territorial)**
* *Anatomy:* The lithe body of a fox with the facial disk, large eyes, and feathered legs of an owl.
* *Behavior:* A nocturnal ambush predator. Moves completely silently. Will stalk the player from the shadows at night.
* *Culture:* Mistwood villagers use Owl-Fox calls as a warning signal.


* **Lynx-Woodpecker (Territorial)**
* *Anatomy:* A climbing feline with a heavy, sharp beak instead of a standard jaw, and thick climbing claws.
* *Behavior:* Clings to the sides of giant trees, dropping down on unsuspecting prey.



### 3. Glimmering Loch

* **Koi-Goat (Common)**
* *Anatomy:* A goat with iridescent, scaled skin, webbed hooves, and trailing, fin-like ears.
* *Behavior:* Semi-aquatic grazers that eat lilypads. Excellent swimmers.
* *Culture:* The primary livestock of Lapis Cove, providing milk and scaled leather.


* **Otter-Heron (Common)**
* *Anatomy:* The long, stilt-like legs of a heron supporting the sleek body and paws of a river otter.
* *Behavior:* Wades in the shallows, using quick paws to snatch fish.


* **Turtle-Crab (Territorial)**
* *Anatomy:* A heavy crab with a smooth, domed turtle shell protecting its back.
* *Behavior:* Highly defensive. Retracts into its shell when attacked, requiring a Hammer to crack open.



### 4. Aetheria Heartlands

* **Bison-Horse (Common/Draft)**
* *Anatomy:* The muscular, humped front and thick fur of a bison, tapering into the long legs and speed of a draft horse.
* *Behavior:* Docile herd animals.
* *Culture:* The backbone of the Heartlands economy; the only creature strong enough to reliably pull heavy merchant caravans.


* **Crow-Hound (Common)**
* *Anatomy:* A sleek hunting dog covered in black feathers, featuring a heavy corvid beak.
* *Behavior:* Highly intelligent and easily trained.
* *Culture:* Used by the Wayfinder Lodges as advanced courier animals for urgent, small parcels.


* **Mole-Ram (Territorial)**
* *Anatomy:* A stout, blind creature with massive digging claws and curled ram horns.
* *Behavior:* Digs extensive tunnel networks. Highly territorial of its burrows.
* *Culture:* Responsible for major road collapses along the Grand Route.

# docs/MOUNTS_AND_PACK_ANIMALS.md

## PURPOSE

To define how players and NPCs transport goods across Aetheria using the world's native hybrid fauna. Standard horses do not exist.

### Beasts of Burden (Pack Animals)

* **Bison-Horse (Caravan Draft):** Too wide and heavy to be ridden by a single Wayfinder effectively, but players will frequently escort merchant wagons pulled by them.
* **Koi-Goat (Ferry-Puller):** Used in the Loch to pull small rafts through the shallow, weed-choked waters where standard paddles get stuck.

### Future Mounts (Player Ridden)

*When mounts are introduced, they must respect the map scale and terrain.*

* **The Ridge-Goat (Derived from Koi-Goat/Mountain Goat):** A smaller, highly agile beast capable of ignoring mud and making small leaps over broken Brass-Line pipes.
* **Giant Stag-Moth:** A rare, tamed variant from the deep woods. While it cannot fly, it can use its massive moth-wings to glide gracefully down from high cliffs, acting as a living paraglider for the player.

# docs/HUNTING_AND_TRACKING_SYSTEM.md

## PURPOSE

To define how the player interacts with wildlife outside of standard self-defense. Hunting is a vital skill for a Wayfinder to gather food and crafting materials.

### Tracking Mechanics

* **Visual Cues:** Crushed grass, scraped bark (from a territorial Badger-Boar), or half-eaten glowing mushrooms (from a Stag-Moth).
* **Aethercraft Integration:** Using the *Echo Sense* skill sends a pulse that temporarily highlights fresh animal tracks or outlines a predator waiting in ambush through thick brush.

### Hunting Gameplay

* **The Sling:** Used to stun smaller prey (Hare-Deer) or knock Finch-Squirrels out of trees without damaging their pelts.
* **The Bow:** Used for lethal takedowns. Requires the player to manage wind direction and crouch to reduce noise.
* **Trapping:** Wayfinders can craft and set snare-traps on identified animal trails, returning the next day to collect the salvage.

### The Salvage Economy

Killing a creature yields no EXP. Instead, it drops physical items:

* **Meats:** Used at campfires to create stamina-boosting meals.
* **Pelts/Feathers/Scales:** Taken to town weavers and blacksmiths to craft upgraded backpacks, weather-resistant coats, and climbing gloves.

# docs/APEX_CREATURES.md

## PURPOSE

Apex Creatures are Aetheria's equivalent of mini-bosses. They are massive, ancient variants of standard hybrids that have survived for decades. They guard rare materials and optional exploration paths.

### 1. The Elder Owl-Fox (Whispering Woods)

* **Description:** A massive, silvery-white variant of the Owl-Fox. Its eyes glow with a piercing blue luminescence.
* **Combat:** An intense game of cat-and-mouse. The Elder Owl-Fox blends perfectly into the night. The player must use their *Spirit Lantern* to reveal it, parry its silent diving attacks, and strike before it vanishes back into the canopy.
* **Rewards:** Elder-Feathers (used to craft the highest-tier stealth cloak) and the "Shadow-Bane" title.

### 2. The Ironshell Turtle-Crab (Glimmering Loch)

* **Description:** A colossal Turtle-Crab whose shell has calcified with heavy iron and ancient Brass-Line debris.
* **Combat:** A puzzle-boss encounter. Standard weapons bounce off. The player must lure the creature over active underwater geysers to flip it onto its back, exposing its vulnerable underbelly.
* **Rewards:** Brass-Plated Carapace (used to forge the ultimate hazard-resistance boots).

### 3. The Ancient Stag-Moth (Verdant Cradle / Forest Border)

* **Description:** A peaceful but imposing giant. Its antlers have grown so large they host their own miniature ecosystems of moss and flowers.
* **Combat:** *Non-lethal.* The Ancient Stag-Moth does not attack. However, to harvest its ultra-rare shedding velvet, the player must successfully climb the moving beast while managing their stamina, similar to a localized platforming puzzle.
* **Rewards:** Ancient Velvet (used to craft the largest capacity Wayfinder Backpack).
